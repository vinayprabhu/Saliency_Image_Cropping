For the past year, we have been investigating this murky yet fascinating tech called Saliency cropping that passively touches all our lives silently influencing the ways in which we encounter digital images on the world wide web.

Sold under different monikers such as â€˜AI assisted croppingâ€™, â€˜Smart croppingâ€™ and  â€˜Content aware croppingâ€™, it has come to be extensively used by all the major digital content serving platforms such as Twitter, Facebook, Google, Microsoft, Apple etc that seek to deliver their image-laden content on a wide array of consumption devices of varying screen sizes, aspect ratios and viewing angles.
